About

I'm a Principal Research Data Scientist at Google Threat Intelligence (GTIG), where I build AI systems for cyber threat intelligence, information operations detection, and adversarial analysis of AI. My work sits at the intersection of frontier AI research and national security.

At Google I've led the design of the organization's first agentic systems for complex cyber threat intelligence, moving from semi-manual workflows through RAG systems to autonomous AI agents. I architected the GTIG Agent Lab and MCP ecosystem using Google's Agent Development Kit (ADK) and Model Context Protocol, enabling autonomous agents to interact with sensitive threat data at scale. My automated narrative tracking technology for information operations campaigns was directly cited in U.S. DOJ enforcement actions against foreign influence operations.

I've developed novel methods for automated analysis across IO campaigns, hacktivist activity, threat actor profiling, propaganda asset tracking, and emerging narrative detection during major geopolitical events including the 2024 U.S. Presidential Election, European elections, the Israel-Hamas conflict, and the Paris Olympics. I also designed the organization's first comprehensive evaluation framework for agentic systems in cyber threat intelligence, replacing subjective manual assessments with auto-rater methodologies and automated regression testing.

Previously I was Director of Applied Science at Graphika, where I led machine learning research on information operations, disinformation, and computational social science. I hold an M.S. in Analytics from Georgia Tech and a B.A. in Psychology (Cognitive Neuroscience) from The New School. I've also taught machine learning and data science at Columbia University and statistics at Baruch College (CUNY).

I run Salt Data Labs, a consulting practice delivering AI/ML solutions and trainings on information operations, disinformation, and machine learning to NGOs, private companies, and government agencies.
